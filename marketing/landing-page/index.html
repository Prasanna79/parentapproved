Built different, on purpose.

Local-first by default

Everything runs on your TV. Your phone connects over WiFi. No cloud. No account. No data leaves your home. Want remote access? Turn it on in Settings — it’s optional, and the relay is free and open-source. You choose. We don’t decide for you.

Open source

Every line of code is on GitHub. Read it, audit it, fork it. We have nothing to hide because there’s nothing to hide.

View on GitHub →

No business model

No subscription. No ads. No “premium tier.” No data to sell. This is a parent side project, not a startup. If you find it useful, consider donating to a children’s charity of your choice.

Single-purpose

This app does one thing: play parent-approved videos on the TV. Currently works with YouTube™ playlists — support for other video sources is planned. It doesn’t track your kid. It just plays what you picked.

Is this for you?

This is for you if…

  • ✓ You have an Android TV (Mi Box, Chromecast with Google TV, Shield, or any Android TV box)
  • ✓ You already know which YouTube™ playlists or channels your kids should watch
  • ✓ You’re comfortable installing an app outside the Play Store (we have a step-by-step guide)
  • ✓ You want zero algorithm involvement in what your kids see on the TV
  • ✓ You want to manage what’s on the TV from your phone, without handing anyone your data

Not for you if…

  • ✗ You want the app to pick “safe” content for you (we don’t curate — you do)
  • ✗ You need Apple TV, Roku, or Fire TV support (Android TV only for now)
  • ✗ You want a polished, Play Store app (this is an early beta — it works, but it’s not fancy)
  • ✗ You’re not comfortable with a short technical setup

No. All data stays on your TV. Playlists, watch history, settings — everything is stored locally in a database on the TV itself. Nothing is sent to any cloud server. Nothing is tracked. Inside the app, there’s no way to search for videos, no settings menu accessible to kids, and no way to browse anything you haven’t added. The Back button returns to the playlist screen. The one thing we can’t prevent: the Home button on the remote exits to the Android TV launcher. That’s an operating system limitation that no app can override. For a dedicated kids’ TV, you can set ParentApproved.tv as the default launcher (guide coming soon).

Yes — if you choose to turn it on. Go to Settings on the TV and enable Remote Access. Your TV connects to a lightweight relay, and your phone’s dashboard works from anywhere. You can add playlists, check what’s playing, pause playback — all from your phone at work, on the bus, wherever. The relay is free and runs as charityware. If you prefer to keep everything fully local, just leave Remote Access off — the app works completely over your home WiFi.

The relay is a tiny open-source bridge that connects your phone to your TV when you’re not on the same WiFi. It forwards your requests to the TV and sends responses back — that’s all it does. It doesn’t store playlists, videos, PINs, or watch history. Your TV is still the backend and all data stays in your TV’s local database. The relay code is open-source too — you can audit it or even host your own.

The TV needs internet to stream videos. But all your playlists and settings are stored locally on the TV — they survive reboots, power outages, and app updates. Nothing depends on a cloud service. If your internet goes out, the playlists are still there when it comes back.

The technology that plays videos occasionally needs updates when YouTube changes something on their end. When this happens, we release an updated version of the app. Sign up for email updates or watch the GitHub repository to get notified. These updates are usually quick — download the new app file and install it over the old one.
